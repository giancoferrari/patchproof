# Code of Conduct

PatchProof is built for careful disagreement: contributors should be able to challenge security assumptions, implementation choices, and evidence claims without attacking one another.

## Expected behavior

Participants are expected to:

- communicate with respect, patience, and technical honesty;
- criticize ideas and code rather than people;
- explain claims with reproducible evidence when possible;
- make room for different backgrounds, experience levels, languages, and communication styles;
- accept good-faith correction and repair harm;
- protect private, security-sensitive, or personally identifying information;
- disclose relevant conflicts of interest;
- respect maintainers' decisions about project scope while remaining free to disagree constructively.

## Unacceptable behavior

The project does not tolerate:

- harassment, threats, stalking, intimidation, or sustained disruption;
- discriminatory or degrading comments, imagery, or conduct;
- sexualized language, imagery, or unwelcome attention;
- personal insults, deliberate humiliation, or publishing private information;
- impersonation, deceptive attribution, or knowingly fabricated evidence;
- pressuring someone to disclose private security details or credentials;
- retaliation against a person who reports a concern in good faith;
- any conduct that would reasonably make participation unsafe.

Technical disagreement, a rejected proposal, or a request for stronger evidence is not harassment when communicated professionally.

## Scope

This code applies in repositories, issues, pull requests, reviews, discussions, release channels, project events, and private communication undertaken on behalf of PatchProof. It also applies when someone publicly represents the project or uses an official project role.

## Reporting conduct concerns

For an ordinary moderation concern, contact the repository maintainers through a GitHub issue with only the information safe to publish. For confidential concerns, use GitHub private vulnerability reporting when available, even if the report is about conduct rather than a software vulnerability. If no private channel is available, open an issue titled `Private conduct contact request` without naming people or describing sensitive events publicly.

Include the relevant dates, location, participants, behavior, available records, and any immediate safety concern. Reports made in good faith will be handled as privately as the platform and investigation allow. Information may be shared with only those needed to assess the report or meet legal/safety obligations.

Do not use the reporting process to make knowingly false allegations or to relitigate a technical decision without a conduct issue.

## Enforcement

Maintainers responsible for moderation may take actions proportional to context, impact, history, and safety, including:

1. a private clarification or request to change behavior;
2. a formal warning and conditions for continued participation;
3. temporary limits on interaction or participation;
4. removal of content or project privileges;
5. a permanent ban from project spaces;
6. escalation to platform administrators or appropriate authorities when necessary.

Immediate restrictions may be used while a serious report is assessed. A maintainer with a conflict of interest must step away from the decision when another suitable reviewer is available. Retaliation is itself a violation.

The project may decline to publish case details in order to protect affected people. Enforcement decisions should record the rule involved and the action taken privately when practical.

## Appeals

A person subject to a formal restriction may request one reconsideration by the maintainers, explaining relevant factual errors, changed circumstances, or disproportionate impact. An appeal does not pause measures needed for safety. Maintainers will review it on a best-effort basis and the resulting project-level decision is final.
